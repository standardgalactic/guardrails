PCM works by converting continuous sound waves into a series of discrete snapshots or samples at regular intervals, which are then stored as binary data. However, this process has two significant drawbacks: temporal (jitter) errors and frequency loss. 

1. **Temporal Error (Jitter):** Due to the fixed sampling rate, PCM can't capture the exact moment when a sound wave's peak amplitude occurs between two sample points. As a result, it must estimate this position, causing a slight temporal error or "jitter." This introduces inaccuracies into the timing of the performance.

2. **Frequency Loss:** To keep file sizes manageable, PCM systems discard frequencies above 20 kHz, which are technically beyond human hearing but contribute to the richness and depth (overtones) of a sound. 

The Phoenix 8A Audio format aims to overcome these limitations by abandoning the digital grid entirely. This process is called "dehydration," where complex audio is separated into distinct, meaningful events - steady harmonic tones from sharp transient attacks. 

These elements are then translated into precise mathematical formulas (rational fractions), preserving all the nuances of the original performance without being constrained by a digital grid. This format allows for perfect playback resolution that scales up with hardware, making recordings immune to the degradation that comes with aging digital formats.

Moreover, this mathematical representation enables new possibilities in post-production. Instead of destructively altering mixed sounds (like traditional equalization or filtering), artists can manipulate isolated variables within the dehydrated file. This means adjustments like increasing vocal vibrato intensity or adding weight to piano harmonics can be made without degrading the original master data, preserving the physical energy and intent of the performance.

In essence, Phoenix 8A Audio format captures the living, interactive acoustics of a performance, ensuring the artist's exact intent is preserved for as long as the music is played, unlike traditional formats that compromise and age over time.
